Supply Chain Demo

Four important actors in the coffee supply chain are the shippers, forwarders, customs officials, and port workers. Use this demo to practice making different types of transactions from these perspectives in order to track a shipping container of coffee beans as it moves across borders and through ports

Exercise: Shipper
Login as Shipper to prepare a shipment of coffee beans. Create a new container by filling in:
  • Container ID: your last name
  • Departure Port: Rotterdam
  • Destination Port: Singapore
  • Cargo: Coffee
  • Container Type: Dry Storage
A digital twin of your container is created on the Tangle and a MAM channel is opened for recording the journey of your container. Click on the Tangle button to see the data related to your container. The status shows the date your container was created. Logout as shipper

Exercise: Forwarder
Login as Forwarder and search for your container by entering your container ID. Click the Confirm Gate-in button. Now, status shows two boxes:
  1. Date shipper created the container
  2. Date forwarder confirmed gate-in
Logout as Forwarder





Exercise: Customs
Login as a customs official and search for your container by entering your container ID Click the Confirm Container Cleared for Export button. Now the status shows three boxes:
  1. Date shipper created the container
  2. Date forwarder confirmed gate-in
  3. Date customs confirmed that the container was cleared for export
Logout as Customs





Exercise: Port
Login as a port official and search for your container by entering your container ID Click the Confirm Container Loaded on Vessel button. Now the status shows four boxes:
  1. Date shipper created the container
  2. Date forwarder confirmed gate-in
  3. Date customs confirmed that the container was cleared for export
  4. Date port confirmed the container was loaded on the vessel
Logout as Port
